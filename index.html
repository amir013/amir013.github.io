About Me

I'm a Master's student in Power Engineering at TU Munich, specializing in Control Systems & Autonomous Robotics. My research sits at the boundary of classical control theory and machine learning — building systems where performance guarantees matter.

Currently, I'm implementing neural network approximations of Nonlinear MPC for rocket engine thrust control, achieving a 1000× inference speedup with 95–99% of optimal performance. I'm also working with real robotic hardware: ROS2-based UAV platforms and the Franka Emika FR3 manipulator.

Based in Munich, Germany. Open to research collaborations, internships, and challenging engineering problems in autonomy and control.
